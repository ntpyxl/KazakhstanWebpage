CULTURE

Nomadic Heritage & Hospitality

Traditional yurts (portable round tents) symbolize nomadic life, and many Kazakhs still embrace them for celebrations.

Hospitality is sacredâ€”guests are treated with great respect and offered food, often beshbarmak (boiled meat with pasta) and kumis (fermented mare's milk).

The dombra, a two-stringed instrument, plays a key role in storytelling and folk music.

Diverse Influences & Modern Identity

A mix of Islamic, Turkic, and Soviet influences shape the nation's architecture, customs, and languages.

While Kazakh and Russian are the official languages, many also speak English in cities.

Modern Kazakhstan balances deep-rooted traditions with futuristic urban development, seen in cities like Astana (Nur-Sultan).

Festivals & Traditions

Nauryz (Kazakh New Year) is the biggest festival, celebrated on March 21 with feasts, games, and cultural performances.

Kokpar, a traditional horseback game similar to polo, is a fierce sport where riders compete for a goat carcass.

Eagle hunting remains a revered tradition, passed down through generations in the Altai Mountains.

ACTIVITIES IN KAZAKHSTAN

Experience Traditional Kazakh Eagle Hunting

Visit the Altai or Almaty regions to witness berkutchi (eagle hunters) in action. Learn how the locals train golden eagles for hunting, a centuries-old tradition.

Stay in a Yurt and Experience Nomadic Culture

Spend a night in a traditional Kazakh yurt in the Altyn-Emel National Park or Kazakh steppes. Participate in traditional games like kokpar (horseback tug-of-war) or watch folk performances. Enjoy authentic Kazakh cuisine like beshbarmak (meat and pasta dish) and kumis (fermented mare's milk).

Ride the Soviet-Era Metro in Almaty

The Almaty Metro is one of the most beautifully decorated subway systems in the world. Each station is uniquely designed with intricate mosaics and Kazakh cultural motifs. It's an affordable and easy way to experience Kazakhstan's urban culture.

Try Ice Fishing on Lake Balkhash

During winter, Lake Balkhash freezes over, making it a prime spot for ice fishing. Join local fishermen and catch species like pike, perch, and catfish. Experience traditional ice-fishing techniques and warm up with local cuisine.

Go Horseback Riding in the Kazakh Steppe

Ride through the endless steppes and valleys of Kazakhstan like a true nomad. Popular regions for horseback riding include Borovoe (Burabay) and the Tien Shan foothills. Multi-day horseback expeditions are available for adventure travelers.
