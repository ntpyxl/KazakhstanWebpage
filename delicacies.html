DELICACIES IN KAZAKHSTAN

Beshbarmak

Known as "Five Fingers", as it's traditionally eaten by hand. A flavorful dish of boiled meat (usually lamb or horse) served over wide pasta sheets, topped with onion broth. A must-try for experiencing authentic Kazakh hospitality!

Manti

Large dumplings filled with seasoned minced lamb or beef. Often served with butter, sour cream, or a spicy tomato sauce. Best enjoyed fresh from a traditional steam pot!

Kazy

A delicacy among nomads, made from seasoned horse rib meat stuffed into natural casing. Boiled or dried, it has a rich and unique flavor. Often served during feasts or alongside Beshbarmak.

Kumis

A mildly alcoholic, sour dairy drink made from fermented mare's milk. Considered nutritious and refreshing, it has been a staple of Kazakh nomads for centuries. Its unique tangy taste may take some getting used to!

Baursak

Small, golden fried dough pieces, similar to donuts but less sweet. Often served with honey, jam, or just plain as a side to tea. A staple at celebrations, weddings, and gatherings.
